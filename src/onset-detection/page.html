Motivation

If you want to learn about onset detection or beat tracking, you will find lots of papers, but little code. Then you will start finding references to a blog series by Bad Logic games. The website is gone but you can find the articles in the wayback machine. Most of the saves are missing the images. Here are the best saves of the two most important articles I've found: Onset Detection Part 6: Onsets & Spectral Flux and Onset Detection Part 7: Thresholding & Peak picking

The articles use Python. It's probably python2 since it was written in 2010. That's fine. Now Web Audio exists. It would be fun to do it in the browser.

Anyways I need onset detection and beat tracking for other things I'm working on. Might as well write it down and save this information for future generations.

What is an onset?

An onset is the beginning of a note. Above is a recoding of a single guitar note. The onset is the tall part on the left. It's actually kind of hard for the computer to find onsets in music. They have contests about it.
